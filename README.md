# Open Source Software and Web Services for Designing Therapeutic Molecules

## Overview

This review paper discusses open-source software, databases, and freely available web services used in computer-aided drug discovery (CADD) and therapeutic molecule design.

The paper focuses on computational tools used in:

- Chemoinformatics
- Pharmacoinformatics
- Drug discovery
- QSAR modeling
- Molecular docking
- ADMET prediction
- Pharmacophore analysis
- Drug target prediction

The study highlights the importance of open-source platforms for reducing the cost and time involved in drug development.

---

## Research Paper

**Title:** Open Source Software and Web Services for Designing Therapeutic Molecules

**Authors:**  
Deepak Singla, Sandeep Kumar Dhanda, Jagat Singh Chauhan, Anshu Bhardwaj, Samir K. Brahmachari, Open Source Drug Discovery Consortium and Gajendra P. S. Raghava

**Journal:** Current Topics in Medicinal Chemistry (2013)

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.2174/1568026611313100005

## Background

Drug discovery is a costly and time-consuming process that may require:

- 10–15 years
- Hundreds of millions of dollars

Computer-Aided Drug Designing (CADD) has emerged as an alternative strategy to:

- Reduce cost
- Improve screening efficiency
- Accelerate lead optimization
- Support virtual screening

The paper emphasizes that freely available computational resources can democratize drug discovery research.
